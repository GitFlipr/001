# Bayes' theorem (theory)

## What it is

Bayes' theorem updates probability given evidence: P(A|B) = P(B|A)P(A)/P(B). Foundation for Bayesian inference: prior → likelihood → posterior.

## When to use it

To interpret posteriors, priors, and likelihoods in models (e.g. HMM, Bayesian regression). When you see "prior" or "posterior": prior = belief before data; posterior = belief after data.

## How it works

Concept/theory; no separate runner. Use when implementing or reading about Bayesian regression, Naive Bayes, or probabilistic strategies.

## Related tests

HMM regime (Bayesian interpretation); permutation, bootstrap (interpretation); VaR/risk updates.
