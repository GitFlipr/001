# Central limit theorem (theory)

## What it is

Sums or averages of many independent random variables tend to a normal distribution. Sample means become approximately normal as n grows.

## When to use it

To justify normal-based confidence intervals and tests. To understand why bootstrap distributions often look normal. For t-tests and large-sample approximations.

## How it works

Concept only; no runner. Use when interpreting sample means or parametric VaR. Prefer permutation and block bootstrap when n is small or data is skewed.

## Related tests

Summary stats; bootstrap; parametric VaR.
